Material choices

Select the perfect stone for your bathroom.

D&C offers granite, marble, and quartz for bathroom vanity tops. Each material brings unique beauty, durability, and maintenance characteristics to your bathroom design.

Granite Vanity Tops

Natural granite offers unmatched durability and distinctive patterns. Each slab is unique, with colors ranging from classic blacks and whites to warm browns, cool grays, and dramatic exotic patterns. Granite resists scratches, heat, and moisture when properly sealed.

Marble Vanity Tops

Marble brings timeless elegance and luxury to bathroom spaces. Classic white Carrara, dramatic Calacatta, or richly veined colored marbles create sophisticated vanity tops. Marble requires more maintenance than granite but offers unparalleled beauty.

Quartz Vanity Tops

Engineered quartz combines beauty with low maintenance. Non-porous, stain-resistant, and available in consistent colors and patterns, quartz is ideal for busy bathrooms. No sealing required—just clean with soap and water.

How it works

The vanity top process.

From slab selection to installation, D&C guides you through each step of creating your custom bathroom vanity top.

1

Visit the Showroom

Come to D&C's Ludlow showroom to view granite, marble, and quartz slabs. Bring vanity cabinet measurements, sink dimensions, and inspiration photos to discuss your project.

2

Select Your Slab & Details

Choose your stone material, select edge profile, specify backsplash style, and confirm sink cutout dimensions. D&C provides a quote and project timeline.

3

Templating

Once cabinets are installed and level, D&C templates your vanity to ensure precise measurements for fabrication. This typically takes 30-45 minutes.

4

Fabrication

D&C cuts, shapes, polishes, and finishes your vanity top at their Ludlow facility. Sink cutouts are made, edges are profiled, and the stone is sealed (granite/marble).

5

Installation

D&C delivers and installs your vanity top, ensuring a level, secure fit. Plumbing connections for sinks and faucets are handled by your plumber.
